How to make and keep Friends

September 9, 2024
Health-Exercise

I am writing this as a means to push it deep into my brains that this is what I need to do in order to make friends and have some company.

Filter People

Well sounds like a bad start, if you dont have a lot of people to interact with who are you to filter them out? But its a necessity, to be with people that get you.

Be yourself, say stuff you think is funny, and be open about things, this will help you filter out the people who you will actually enjoy having around you.

Be Unique

Dont be a NPC, you want to be remembered, be quirky, talk about that weird interest (obviously don't overload someone with a lot information), be memorable. Be the guy who talks about how he knows how to work on medieval torture devices. For me it would probably be making a game world with a super rich story and deep lore like human history level.

Stay out of your head

Don't freaking overthink. If you are talking in a group, don't waste your time thinking about what you are going to ask, or say, just come out and say it, sure it might get weird, but its a group, people will get over it fast (Worst Case Scenario). Have some confidence in yourself.

Don't Force yourself

No need to force yourself if things don't work out. Its fine, you need to keep trying and not lose confidence.

Be the Main Character

Initiate the conversations, no one will do it for you, it is something you want and in this life the only way to get things is to act on your wants. Put yourself out there, I know it isn't so easy but once you do it a few times, it won't be as hard either, you just need to do it. Actively listen to people you talk to, ask them about things, don't just keep blabbering about your own things, its a conversation. Listening and giving them a chance is a best way to make sure they come back for more later if need be.

Once you are in, you are the Side Character

Take a step back, now you are in a show, make the person you are talking to feel like the main character, let them have the spotlight. Make them feel important, compliment them, say their name when you initiate a conversation, act happy when they walk into the room, remember things about them.

You are the side character of this stage play, make the main character shine, and help them feel important.

Be Transparent

You have to be more open about yourself. You need to be a bit vulnerable at times, and not just give shallow meaningless replies. This helps create trust.

Injury Update

People are wired to respond positively to someone asking about their injuries or things troubling them. Ask them about deeper things, like whats something they've been thinking about lately, did they have any incident lately, show that you care. Give them a space to feel comfortable.

Group Chat

If you had fun with a group of people, ask them about making a group chat. This will give you chances to plan something similar again with the group.

Do people Favors

No dummy you aren't doing this to make them indebted to you. You are doing this let them know that they can depend on you if they need something, building trust in you.

Ask them for Favors

You can't just keep doing them favors, you have to ask them for some too, you want to show you trust them as much as they can trust you. Don't overdo it though then it'll look like you are using them but do do it.

Pick Up Line

Learn a few questions / compliments that can be used in a wide variety of situation with a wide variety of replies. This is a hack to break the ice and get the convo going, and you can keep reusing it with new people.

Go for the next most isolated person

They are the easiest targets to tackle when talking to, they are as confused as you are.

Act like you are already friends

This is the trickiest part of this all but the gains are as good.

If you go looking to find friends you'll find very few. But if you go looking to be a friend, you'll find many

How to act like a friend though? Light up when you meet them, have light banter, say their name, pat them on their neck. Disclaimer this shit can lead you to feel like a creep too...

Activity Buddy

Ask them what they've been wanting to do for sometime and offer to do it with them.
