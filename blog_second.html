A little lookback at the year 2022

Well weirdly enough, I liked rambling on text after my first post, it feels a bit nice I dont really know why or how. But this one that I am going to write as of now would be a review or a look back at my year. I would like to keep it as chronological as it can be so lets get to it.

The first phase of the 2022 (Jan - April) - Well looking back it at now, that was the start of my first offline college life, a new way of living after living with my parents for about 18 years. I was scared, not that I would not be able to handle myself I did not really care or gave much thought to that, I knew one way or the other I would get beaten (not literally) and learn shit either the hard or the easy way that doesnt matter. I was scared about fitting in, now as some context for new people I have never lived at one place for more than 2 years (except jodhpur, but we dont count that) so I was never able to create deep connections and get good friends in my life as a kid I never really thought about it and was quite good at "faking" and fitting in places. Comes in my 3 - 4 years of absolutely no physical friend arc, and lo and behold I realised how bloody hard it is to make friends and my social anxiety and everything else sky rocketed, and I realised I never had any close friend ever. (well we are getting side tracked lets get back to it shall we) So yea I was telling you about me being scared, I was scared of making new friends, getting people I feel safe around and happy around. But it seemed I was scared for naught, I met some people got to know a lot of them, I forgot that since I am in a college everyone is near my age, and there are a lot of people I can connect to. This was the phase of the year where I got the reassurance on the side from the friend side. Well coming to the academic stuff happening, I bombed it, not in the sense I did good in the sense I did so so shit. Kind of saw it coming (should've made the reports ;-;). Along with that I finally found what I wanted to do with my life, was it to go into industry or go into higher studies, and what to actually choose my field to be. At start of college life I was actually attracted to the Electrical engineering stuff (I still like it but more from a guy standing and learning about it and not working in it pov) and was hoping to make my life in it, but then after working some time in game dev I realised I am really into low level coding and want to as a first step get into computer graphics. My initial plan was grand it was to make a game engine (kind of still is but I gave up on the idea of it happening so easily), and started seeing how to get to it. Well with the tests and all, I didnt really got to make much progress in that but I had made my mind what to do. Some small moments of happiness that I had that sem was just hanging out and going to coffee at night with friends, barely attend classes and just hanging out with them in general.

The second phase of 2022 (May - July) - Well with the start of summer vacations and me starting my first research intern (yea I forgot to mention that I got a research intern in first phase) I was off to a very happy start, I had big hopes and lots of plans. Well lets say the plans just stayed plan and I actually did absolutely nothing during this phase of my life, I became lazier and lazier, I barely left my room I watched anime, barely did any research intern work. Why you may ask? Its pretty simple I was too much in my comfort zone and no one actually was pushing me or keeping me in check. This phase will take me the least time to summarize as it was literally just me doing nothing. I thought that the project will be easy and I will be able to do a lot of work, but as soon as I started reading the papers and seeing the code my eyes were opened to one really big thing, computer graphics is an extremely demanding and high entry requiring field. As compared to AI/ML the entry level knowledge you need just to start playing around with the stuff is a lot and really intimidating, and with no guidance and no clear path of where to start, I staggared and just stopped. I tried to get my head around the code once every month and same with the papers, but the difficulty coupled with my infinite laziness made me yeild barely any result. Heck I even gained a lot of weight in this time. Yes in short this phase was my hikkikomori phase doing nothing and not leaving my room. Sorry to dissapoint you the next phase isn't much of a change either. Small fun things about this phase - I got to meet some relatives I have been wanting to meet and some friends too, but nothing too much, was mostly a dull phase, with barely any ups and downs.

The third phase of 2022 (August - Nov) - Well one would say I learnt something from that phase and made some changes, well my thoughts certainly learnt something and tried their best to change things, they made a plan to attend every class and put some effort for once in life. My actions on the other hands had other plans of their own. It did entirely nothing, the first week I attended the classes but slept at the first bench cause I wasnt used to being awake at 8am, the second week I stopped attending any classes, yes they were taking attendance, did I care? It seemed not. Then by the time of mid sems I had lost all kinds of motivation in everything, and was now even trying to find ways to sleep through the lab classes. I would like to blame out on the burning sweaty weather that kills your mind and the infinite amount of insects that entered my room everyday for that, but all the blame lies on me. I was still doing my game dev job on the side, before the guy who hired me decided to stop it all to make those bad mobile apps, and pause my work indefinitely, so there went my stable income too. As for the research project I was actually making very slow progress on that, but with my sleep schedule, every night coffee detours, spending nights in Tiddy's (a friend, yes he is a guy) room doing absolutely nothing. For every exams grinding just 5hrs before the paper, with no shame in us, hoping for a good result. This was yet another shitty phase, well I wont call it as shitty as my second arc of this year, but shitty as in I did absolutely nothing and begged for things like a beggar, and one thing that I have realised is that in life if you want something you have to fight for it and get it, so I didnt get a single thing I wanted. My academic result was once again shit, I passed everything (Yes I am kind of happy about it) but now my chances for a masters is low, so thats that. I still have no regrets, if you put me back in that situation I would do the same thing again, but in hindsight I shouldve not. I started making a list for upcoming intern applications to professors for intern opportunities next summer break. The highlight of this phase would be making some new friends outside my already existing sphere, and gaining an new everyday person in my life, now I have about 2 - 3 of those, which I see as very nice. Small happy things for this phase - well once again it was those small walks to the get coffee, spending time in Tiddy's room doing nothing and just relaxing.

The last phase of 2022 (Dec) - One would think why am I giving one month one whole phase, well let me tell you why. This was the grind arc for my whole year, I decided and enforced change in my life and decided enough was enough, I had to put in effort in my life I wanted to get something done and I acted on it, I started going to my lab and forced myself to work, and believe me it was one of the best feeling this whole year, working on something I enjoy. I started coding stuff and this was it, I realised what I have been missing on by being lazy and not doing shit, I still find it hard for myself to push myself to start something, but I learnt a lot about myself and how to get myself to work. I realised that I need a clear path very clear path from going from A to B and a clear end goal in mind, specially with small enough goals for it. I started coding my main goal was to create my own ray tracer (ended up making a Path tracer but whatever), and to use optix to make it to learn it in the process, as it will help me with my research intern project I mentioned above. I started I debugged I got stuck for days I kept trying and trying and kept working and well the end goal was beautiful I created a path tracer first on a CPU, and then on the GPU using Optix, it was amazing the feeling of creating something, coding it, the whole process to me was a way to redeem myself and I felt reawakened. I decided to push myself this much in the upcoming sem too, and keep up with the work, and get some results on the intern, specially as a thanks to the professor who has had the patience to guide me through all the 3 phases of this year. Besides this I met up with a few friends, went to my first funeral, and just relaxed back at home, it would've been perfect if I was able to meet my parents but seems one can't ask for everything can they. Oh how did I forgot to add the negative parts? Well here they are I applied for Mitacs, I applied to the most dumb and useless projects in mitacs but as luck would have it I got nothing that was the first punch in the gut, so many seemed to have gotten projects projects that werent of any importance, people with no absolute research experience, who have never as much as read a reserach paper, or done actual work got projects and started flexing, well lets just not dwell on it too much. Besides that was the academic result, well I did not have much hopes but I bombed it again (not the good bomb) as usual it was worse than most of my friends (I said most okay? If you know who I am reffering to I am sorry for you too). My dad also forced me to contact a lot of new people, which helped me expand my view of things and helped to learn about the working of higher studies and workings of jobs in USA much more better. I learned the importance of creating new connections and keeping them active. I met one side of my family that I barely meet and tried building some connections there, lets see how that goes. Well that was it I guess for this phase.

Some final words for the whole blog, this year has been a decent one, I made friends some really nice friends, I got to know of my shortcomings, I realised my standing in stuff (Believe me it is not good I have a lot of work to do and explore a bit in life too, I want to explore different fields related to computers, not related to computers and a lot more) I have to start applying to more places, build more connections, read lots of papers and push myself way way more. I have to work on myself, do some concrete work to increase my value in this world, and just push a little more with academics and health. Yes there might be some inconsistency with a lot of the blog, but thanks for reading till here. That was about it for my year, I need to be a bit more active but lets see.
